Anderson Holderness has strong memories of the beach where he grew up. Approximately 80% of his dreams are located in and around a dream version of Wilmington, North Carolina. The dreams are haunting, violent, humorous, or all three.

Once, Anderson saw a killer whale surge from the surf, bite a man's leg, and drag him back into the sea. Another time Anderson had a buffalo for a pet dog, and they played frisbee on the beach all day long—every so often he remembers its sturdy head and its sandy, matted fur. Another time he was sloshing around in ankle deep water when a bear attacked him. As he fought back, the bear disintegrated in his hands, into a blanket he clutched as he woke.

Dreams are emotional geography. Anderson enjoys probing the vast, undulating world of his subconscious, and mapping and charting where his visions take him.
